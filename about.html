ABOUT DHARAVI

Dharavi is one of the world's largest slums. It has an area of 2.1 square kilometers and is home to more than one million people. However, in defiance of the usual stereotypes of stagnation and poverty, Dharavi is home to a bustling economy, valued at more than $1 billion. Its entrepeneurs, youth, and artisans are helping create a better tomorrow with their resilience and innovation. Click on the videos below to get a glimpse of life in Dharavi.

FUN FACTS

  • Historical Island: In the 18th century, Dharavi was actually an island.
  • Young Innovators: A couple of 13-14 year old girls from Dharavi have created Android apps, including one that notifies users about their turn to fetch water from the communal tap.
  • Economic Powerhouse: Despite its reputation as a slum, Dharavi has a bustling economy with an annual turnover of over USD 650 million.
  • Recycling Hub: The recycling units in Dharavi not only process waste from Mumbai but from all over the country and even abroad, contributing significantly to the economy.
  • Cultural Melting Pot: Dharavi is home to a diverse population with people from various religious, ethnic, and linguistic backgrounds living together.
